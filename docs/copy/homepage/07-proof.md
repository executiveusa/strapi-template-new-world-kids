# Section 07. Proof

Source: `apps/ui/src/components/homepage/ProofSection.tsx`

## Heading

- Trust comes from promises kept in public.

## Intro

- Seth Godin's framework is useful here: make a real promise, help people become someone more capable, and keep that promise when it is hard.
- The site now reflects that instead of burying it in nonprofit filler.

## Trust signals

### Concrete promise

- We do not promise vague transformation.
- We promise usable skills that help young people grow food, save water, generate energy, and build shelter.

### Public proof

- The site, the timeline, the budget framing, and the Hermes status feed all point back to one rule: show the work, then ask for trust.

### Funding clarity

- Programs stay free to students while revenue, grants, and donor support cover the operating load behind the scenes.

## Testimonials

- Parents who were skeptical about another youth program changed their minds when they saw students engage differently here.
- We don't have polished testimonials yet. We have 5 years of showing up, 200 plant varieties growing, and kids who come back every week because they want to. The proof is in the soil.
- Early program feedback keeps returning to the same point: the curriculum feels practical, creative, and grounded in lived experience.
