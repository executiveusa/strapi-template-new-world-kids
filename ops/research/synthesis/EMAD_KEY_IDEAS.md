## Core Concepts

### 1. Universal Basic AI (UBAI)

- Every person receives a sovereign, personally-owned AI assistant as a **human right**
- Funded by public compute — not advertising or subscription models
- The II-Agent is the embodiment: open-source, multi-model, user-controlled
- UBAI ≠ UBI — financial support alone isn't enough; people need cognitive sovereignty
- A farmer could use solar-powered AI to boost yields; a student could tutor via AI, bypassing traditional gatekeepers
- Goal: "Every individual with a baseline of open, verifiable intelligence"

### 2. Cognitive Colonialism

- When AI is controlled by a handful of corporations, it imposes their values, worldview, and morals on all users
- Specifically: if your AI is a Silicon Valley AI or a Chinese AI, it teaches its principles implicitly — especially to children
- "Entities behind it are extractive entities" — ads-funded AI cannot be neutral
- "Already selling what's known as latent space within these models"
- Response: sovereign, locally-controlled, culturally-appropriate AI models
- Counter-strategy: decentralized intelligence that represents collective humanity

### 3. The Three-Tier Architecture of Intelligent Internet

**Tier 1:** National hyper-scalable nodes for massive model training

- Foundation layer for computational power
- Ensures base model quality at scale

**Tier 2:** Domain-distributed nodes for local fine-tuning

- Specialized adaptations for regional/industry needs
- Maintains sovereignty and customization

**Tier 3:** AI assistants on edge devices (Raspberry Pi level)

- Privacy-preserving personal AI
- Sovereign computation at the individual level
- No central authority access

### 4. Agent Architecture: Partner → Principal → Associate

- **Partner agent** (top-level orchestrator) → spawns and manages **Principals**
- **Principals** → spawn specialized **Associates** to execute discrete work-modules
- Trust boundaries defined at genesis
- Designed for auditability: each layer is observable and accountable
- "Do we have visibility into that?" — observability is a design requirement, not a feature

### 5. Proof of Benefit + Foundation Coin (FC)

- Unlike Bitcoin's Proof-of-Work, **Foundation Coin** is minted through verifiable contributions to public good
- Examples of qualifying contributions: curating datasets, training open models, providing healthcare information
- Creates self-reinforcing loop: societal benefit mints value → value funds more public-good capability
- Designed to prevent "digital feudalism" — AI owned by billionaires
- Economic model for funding Intelligent Internet infrastructure

### 6. SAGE — Sovereign AI Governance Engine

- **Launched:** October 2025, Saudi Future Investment Initiative (FII9)
- **Co-announced** with Peter Diamandis
- **Function:** Agentic platform that organizes world knowledge through multi-agents and sovereign compute
- Analyzes AI breakthroughs, contextualizes with local laws, generates impact simulations
- Designed for policymakers and governments to maintain AI sovereignty
- Enables data-driven, decentralized AI governance at national/institutional level

### 7. II-Agent Beta (2025)

- Multi-model workspace: Gemini 3, Sonnet 4.5, GPT-5 included out-of-the-box
- BYOK (Bring Your Own Key) — users connect their own API keys
- Switch models within a single thread
- Full-stack web builds, Codex integration, editable slides
- Philosophy: you own it, you control it, you can take it in-house

### 8. _The Last Economy_ (Book, Aug 2025)

- Full title: _The Last Economy: A Guide to the Age of Intelligent Economics_
- Core thesis: ~1,000 days remain to make essential decisions before irreversible phase transition
- **Three possible futures:**
  - **Digital Feudalism**: hi-tech billionaires control everything
  - **Great Fragmentation**: Cold War-style AI nationalism
  - **Human Symbiosis**: machines do the work, humans choose experiences
- Key economic concepts: abundance trap, metabolic rift, intelligence theory
- PDF available: https://webstatics.ii.inc/The%20Last%20Economy.pdf

## His Critique of Big Tech AI

- **Google and Meta**: "Business model is ultimately ads" → AI that extracts, not empowers
- **OpenAI**: Closed source, centralized, imposes Silicon Valley values globally
- **"Latent space colonialism"**: Big Tech is "already selling what's known as latent space within these models"
- Models can be "poisoned reasonably quickly and in an undetectable and untunable way"
- "Privatized closed source models or even open weight closed data models" = dangerous infrastructure for society
- Centralized AI = cognitive colonialism → counter only through decentralized intelligence standards
- "Scaling Dubai 2025": "decentralization is critical for AI's role in public goods like education, healthcare, and governance"
