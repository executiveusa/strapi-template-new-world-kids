Energy is the pillar people often underestimate until it disappears.

Without reliable power, lights go out, refrigeration breaks, connectivity fails, and the day becomes smaller than it should be. For a project trying to join field work with modern tools, energy is freedom.

## Why energy belongs in the same story

Food, water, energy, and shelter are not separate marketing buckets. They reinforce each other.

Reliable power helps:

- preserve food
- run communications tools
- support learning materials
- keep reporting and publishing alive

In other words, energy helps the mission stay legible.

## The operating model

The materials in this repo describe a practical off-grid stack:

- solar
- biogas and biodigester thinking
- low-voltage power systems
- connectivity layers such as Starlink

The point is not to sound futuristic. The point is to build continuity in places where continuity is precious.

## Why this matters for the site

A strong nonprofit site should explain energy in plain language. Donors do not need a technical whitepaper before they believe in the work. They need clarity.

The right sequence is:

1. explain the human consequence
2. name the system
3. show how support helps
4. offer proof for anyone who wants to dig deeper

## The AI angle

Energy also supports the media and AI layer of New World Kids. If the organization is going to publish field intelligence, run a transparent operations backend, and provide useful services to the community, the power layer has to be real.

That is why this pillar belongs both in the mission and in the business model.
