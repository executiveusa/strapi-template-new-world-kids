The timeline matters because it answers a donor question quickly: is this still an idea, or has the work already started becoming real?

For New World Kids, the answer is that the work is already in motion.

## What the first seasons prove

The site did not begin with polished infrastructure. It began with damaged soil, rented land, and people willing to keep showing up.

That early phase matters because it proves the project was not assembled backwards. The story did not come first. The labor did.

## What the current season proves

Today, the strongest visible proof is the living food system itself:

- the land is producing
- the planting logic is maturing
- the educational frame is becoming clearer
- the operations layer is being organized around grants, reporting, and follow-through

That is a stronger fundraising position than a speculative build because there is already something to point to.

## What still needs backing

The next layers are the ones donors can help unlock faster:

- water systems
- resilient energy systems
- shelter and learning-space buildout
- documentation strong enough to support grants and public trust

This is exactly why the timeline belongs near the top of the site. It closes the gap between mission language and visible evidence.
