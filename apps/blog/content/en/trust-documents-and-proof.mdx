Good intentions do not remove the need for proof.

If we want supporters to move from curiosity to action, they should not have to hunt for the basics: who receives funds, what documents exist, and whether the public story matches the operational reality.

## What trust should feel like on the site

Trust should not be a hidden page for skeptical people. It should be part of the core product experience.

That means:

- fiscal sponsor language should be visible
- key documents should be one click away
- donation paths should be clearly labeled
- stories should connect back to proof instead of floating alone

## Why this matters for the journal

A field journal without verification can feel like branding.

A trust center without human stories can feel sterile.

The two are stronger together. The journal shows the work in motion. The documents prove the organization can be trusted with money and attention.

## Standard going forward

Every major update should be able to link to:

- the relevant pillar
- the next support action
- the trust center
- at least one concrete artifact, image set, or operating note
