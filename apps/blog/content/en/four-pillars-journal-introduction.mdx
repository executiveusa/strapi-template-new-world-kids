Most nonprofit sites hide the real logic of the work under general promises. We are trying to do the opposite.

This journal is built around four plain questions:

1. Can people eat?
2. Can they keep and move water?
3. Can they make or manage useful energy?
4. Can they build and maintain shelter?

Those four pillars are simple enough for a donor to understand quickly and concrete enough for a student to practice with their hands.

## Why the structure matters

When a site gets too abstract, trust drops. People may like the vibe, but they do not know what they are backing.

That is why the journal, the timeline, the trust center, and the donation flow all need to reinforce one another:

- the journal explains the work in public
- the timeline shows when major changes happened
- the trust center proves we are real
- the donation flow makes support immediate once the logic clicks

## What belongs in each pillar

Food includes the forest, propagation, soil recovery, and neighborhood food literacy.

Water includes capture, storage, irrigation, and the discipline of designing around scarcity.

Energy includes off-grid power, resilience, and the tools required to keep a living site operating.

Shelter includes natural building, repair, climate response, and spaces where learning can actually happen.

## What this makes possible

A donor can scan the top layer in seconds, then peel back more detail only if they want to.

That onion structure matters. It respects busy visitors while still giving serious supporters a path into the deeper proof.

## Next move

As this journal fills out, every pillar should hold real photography, field updates, related documents, and a simple path to donate or ask better questions.
